# Code Of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want OneMind to be a careful, rigorous, and humane research space. We pledge
to make participation respectful and constructive for people of all backgrounds,
identities, and levels of experience.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind, precise, and intellectually honest.
- Critique ideas strongly without attacking people.
- Assume good faith, while still naming concrete risks and mistakes.
- Respect privacy, consent, and confidentiality.
- Mark speculation as speculation.
- Help keep the project readable to contributors from multiple disciplines.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private, personal, sensitive, confidential, or non-consensual
  information.
- Doxxing or attempting to identify people from abstracted examples.
- Pushing for real-world surveillance, coercion, or data collection that
  violates the project's safety boundaries.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions after maintainers have asked for focus.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or limit
participation when behavior harms the project or its contributors.

To report a conduct concern, email `shivam@shivamk3r.com`. Reports will be
handled as privately as practical.
